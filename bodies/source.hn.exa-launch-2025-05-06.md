Launch positioning:
- Exa launched Websets, an embeddings-powered search engine returning precise results for complex queries.
- Websets performs dozens of embedding searches over Exa’s vector database, then runs agentic workflows to verify that each result matches the user’s criteria.
- Websets are tables, not lists; users can add enrichment columns and cells asynchronously load.

Founder comments / product evolution:
- Will Bryk says Exa evolved from 2022 consumer-facing embeddings search under Metaphor, to 2023 web search/crawling API for AIs, to Websets as a product on top of search tech.
- He says Exa is fully devoted to improving Websets quality and building the best general search engine in the world.
- He states best-supported surfaces at that time: people, companies, papers, high-quality written content; weak surfaces include products/ecommerce, auth-gated content, and non-English content.

Community feedback themes:
- Positive: high potential for lead generation, vendor/company/person research, “web as database”, alternative to generic deep research walls of text.
- Negative: latency, UI feedback, mobile/table layout, pricing for high-volume use, result quality on some constrained queries, login gating clarity.

Interpretation: this is a much stronger community validation source than a normal HN mention, because Exa got substantial discussion and founder responses surfaced both the strategy and limitations.
