# Sequoia podcast: Pricing in the AI Era

S2 podcast/transcript source, fetched 2026-07-08 via `pinixc browser read`.

Important ideas from Manny Medina:
- Best AI application companies are “hedgehog” businesses: narrow, high-friction workflows rather than broad platforms.
- Strong early AI use cases often replace BPO-heavy or hard-to-backfill labor pools, not necessarily the highest-paid jobs first.
- Pricing maturity curve: activity-based -> workflow-based -> outcome-based -> per-agent / human-equivalent pricing.
- Paid is described by Manny as the business engine for AI companies: billing, invoicing, monetization, pricing, collections, revenue recognition, vendor management, and margin management.
- First wedge: monetization engine and margin management engine.
- Paid’s origin came from Manny’s experience at Outreach trying to understand agent margins, value added, and business fundamentals.
- Paid’s onboarding at the time was manual; early-stage team helped customers configure monetization and margin trails.

Research interpretation: this transcript is one of the best sources for Paid’s strategy. Paid’s product direction is broader than billing: it wants to be the operating layer for agent businesses, starting with pricing/margin because that pain is immediate.
