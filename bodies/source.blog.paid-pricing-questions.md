# Paid pricing questions article

S1 official Paid blog, fetched 2026-07-08 via `pinixc browser read`.

Notable facts and ideas:
- Paid says it ran monetization workshops in San Francisco with A16z, Battery Ventures, and General Catalyst for 30+ founders.
- Recommends an 80/20 transition model: keep existing subscription, add included credits, let power users expand by buying more.
- Advises starting credit weights with t-shirt sizing rather than over-engineered precision.
- Emphasizes translating credit amounts into human-equivalent value in sales conversations.
- Frames variable usage as the company’s cost-management problem, not necessarily a pricing model to pass directly to customers.
- States customer success changes from retention management to ROI attribution under credit models.
- Raises unresolved question: what happens when software starts buying software, not humans?

Interpretation: this is GTM/operating doctrine. Paid is trying to own the conversation around pricing design, not just sell billing software.
